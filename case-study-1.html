Quoting at the Speed of Trust

Redesigning an agent-facing insurance quoting experience to reduce friction, build confidence, and cut completion time by 40%.

Role

Lead UX Designer

Timeline

6 months, 2023

Tools

Figma, Miro, UserZoom

Impact

40% faster completion

01 — Context & Challenge

A 12-step process designed for the system, not the human.

Insurance agents spend a significant portion of their day generating product quotes for clients. The existing flow had evolved organically over years — layering on requirements, compliance checkpoints, and edge-case handling until what remained was a process that demanded 12 discrete steps to produce a single quote.

The problem wasn't just speed. Agents reported feeling uncertain about whether they'd made errors, frequently abandoning sessions mid-flow, and spending disproportionate time re-entering data that should have carried forward automatically. Trust in the system had eroded alongside their confidence.

"I feel like I'm fighting the tool rather than serving the client."
— Insurance agent, discovery interview

02 — Research & Discovery

Getting into the real rhythm of the job.

I began with contextual inquiry — sitting alongside agents during their actual quoting sessions, observing where they paused, where they looked away from the screen, and where they reached for workarounds they'd developed on their own.

Key findings from 14 shadowing sessions and 8 structured interviews:

  • 67% of agents kept a parallel spreadsheet to track data before entering it into the system
  • The most common abandonment point was step 7 — a coverage configuration screen with no contextual guidance
  • Agents making errors rarely received immediate feedback; errors surfaced at submission, requiring restart
  • High performers had developed personal workarounds that lowered their cognitive load — none of which were documented or shared

03 — Design Process

Collapsing complexity without losing control.

The key insight was that the 12 steps weren't 12 distinct decisions — they were 12 moments of data entry organized around the database schema, not around how agents actually think about a quote. Regrouping them by mental model collapsed the flow to 4 adaptive stages: Client, Coverage, Review, Submit.

I ran three rounds of prototype testing with 6 agents each, progressively refining the information hierarchy, inline validation behavior, and data persistence logic. Each round surfaced specific issues that could be isolated, addressed, and re-tested without disrupting the broader flow.

04 — Outcomes

Faster, more confident, measurably better.

Post-launch metrics over a 60-day observation period:

  • 40% reduction in average quote completion time
  • 32% improvement in agent satisfaction scores (post-task survey)
  • Mid-flow abandonment dropped from 28% to 9%
  • Error-related re-submissions reduced by 61%

Beyond the numbers, agents reported a qualitative shift — the tool felt like it was working with them, not against them. That trust translated directly into faster client service and, for the business, higher quote conversion rates.
