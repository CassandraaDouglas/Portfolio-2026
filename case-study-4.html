Zero to Prototype

Building an AI-assisted design workflow that takes an idea from brief to validated, interactive prototype in under 48 hours.

Role

Creative Technologist

Timeline

Ongoing, 2024–2025

Tools

Claude, Cursor, Framer, Figma

Impact

4× faster cycles

01 — Context & Challenge

The gap between a good idea and a testable prototype was too wide.

Design teams are routinely asked to validate ideas before they're funded — but the cost of building a prototype worth testing has historically required significant time, resources, and design expertise to produce something interactive enough to generate real signal.

The result is a familiar bottleneck: great ideas die in slide decks, stakeholder decisions get made on static screens nobody truly believes in, and the "fast" path of just shipping something untested costs far more downstream. I wanted to find out how much of that gap could be closed with the right tools, the right prompts, and a clear-headed methodology.

The hypothesis: combining AI code generation, AI design reasoning, and lightweight usability testing could collapse a 2–3 week process into 48 hours — without sacrificing the quality of insight.

02 — Methodology

Designing the workflow, not just using the tools.

The workflow I developed has four phases, each with a distinct AI role:

  • Synthesis (Hour 0–4): Claude as a research partner — processing brief materials, surfacing user need patterns, generating a concise problem frame
  • Architecture (Hour 4–12): Claude + Figma — information architecture, flow logic, and annotated wireframes generated iteratively through structured prompting
  • Build (Hour 12–36): Cursor + Framer — interactive prototype built with AI pair programming; full responsive behavior, realistic interactions, production-quality fidelity
  • Validate (Hour 36–48): Maze or guerrilla sessions — 5-participant usability study, synthesized findings, actionable next iteration

03 — What I Learned

AI doesn't replace judgment — it amplifies it.

The most important finding from running this workflow across multiple projects: the quality of AI output is directly proportional to the quality of the problem framing going in. Vague briefs produce vague prototypes. Sharp thinking upstream produces remarkable things downstream.

The workflow also revealed where human judgment is irreplaceable — deciding which direction to pursue, recognizing when a technically correct output misses the emotional truth of a user need, and synthesizing usability findings into decisions rather than just a list of observations.

04 — Outcomes

A repeatable methodology, not a one-off stunt.

  • 4× compression in prototype delivery time compared to traditional workflow
  • Validated prototypes delivered in under 48 hours across 6 distinct product briefs
  • Usability testing at prototype stage (vs. post-build) surfaced critical issues 3× earlier in the product cycle
  • Methodology documented and shared with 3 additional design teams; adopted as a sprint accelerator

This work reshaped how I think about the role of a designer in 2025 and beyond. The constraint isn't creativity — it's time and translation. AI collapses both, but only when the designer brings the systems thinking to direct it.
