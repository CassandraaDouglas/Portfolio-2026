Chubb Everyday: Agentic Prototype

Building a proof-of-concept for Chubb Everyday, a unified D2C insurance platform, using an entirely agentic workflow from first commit to deployed site in 1 week.

Role

Lead UX Designer

Timeline

1 week, 2025

Tools

Claude Code · Design System MCP · Surge

Status

POC deployed

01 — Context & Challenge

Every D2C product lived somewhere different. Nothing lived together.

Chubb's direct-to-consumer products had grown over time without a shared home. Each product existed in its own silo, with separate experiences, separate entry points, and no unified identity for customers navigating what Chubb had to offer directly.

The vision was Chubb Everyday: a single platform that brought all D2C insurance products together under one roof, with a consistent experience and a clear value proposition. The challenge wasn't just design. It was demonstrating that the concept was viable, fast enough to make the case before the idea lost momentum.

The real question wasn't whether the idea was good. It was whether I could show it compellingly enough, quickly enough, to move from conversation to consideration.

02 — My Role & Process

I didn't build a deck. I built the thing.

Rather than producing a static presentation or a clickable Figma prototype, I used a fully agentic workflow to build and deploy a functional proof-of-concept. The entire project, from design through development and deployment, ran through Claude Code, with Chubb's own Design System MCP connected directly into the workflow.

This meant the POC wasn't approximating the design system. It was built with it. Colors, typography, spacing, and component behavior were all sourced from the real tokens and patterns Chubb uses in production. The result felt like a real product because, structurally, it was built like one.

The POC covers the core D2C journeys: product discovery, quoting flows, and the claims process. These are the three moments that matter most to a customer in their relationship with an insurer. Each flow drew on patterns and decisions from my previous D2C work at Chubb, synthesized into a single coherent experience for the first time.

I included a theme switcher (accessible via a gear icon) to demonstrate the design system's flexibility across Chubb's color palette. It shows stakeholders how the experience could adapt across product lines without breaking the underlying structure. Once the build was complete, I deployed to Surge within a day.

03 — What I'd Do Differently

What's scoped out is the next conversation, not a flaw.

This was a proof-of-concept by design, intentionally scoped to demonstrate viability, not production completeness. The happy path works. What it doesn't yet have is the depth of edge case coverage a live product would require.

To take this to production, I'd want dedicated refinement calls with the product team to pressure-test the flows, and significantly more time to build out the edge cases: error states, accessibility corner cases, and cross-device behavior that a real user will eventually find.

The POC proved the concept. The next phase is proving the details. That's a different kind of work, and I'm ready for it.

04 — Outcomes & Impact

A deployed POC, a new workflow, and a bet on what's next.

Chubb Everyday went from concept to deployed proof-of-concept in 1 week, with the live site shipped to Surge in under a day.

Beyond the product itself, this project demonstrated something broader. A designer with the right agentic tools can collapse the design-to-deploy cycle in ways that fundamentally change how early-stage concepts get evaluated. No waiting for engineering bandwidth. No static mockups that require imagination to fill in the gaps. The thing speaks for itself.

  • Covers full D2C journeys: product discovery, quoting flows, and claims process
  • Built using Chubb's real design system tokens, not approximations
  • Agentic workflow: Claude Code, Design System MCP, and Surge
